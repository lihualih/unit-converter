About Temperature Conversion

Temperature conversion is one of the most commonly needed calculations. Unlike other unit conversions, temperature scales don't simply multiply by a factor — they use formulas with offsets.

Conversion Formulas

  • Celsius to Fahrenheit: °F = °C × 9/5 + 32
  • Fahrenheit to Celsius: °C = (°F − 32) × 5/9
  • Celsius to Kelvin: K = °C + 273.15
  • Kelvin to Celsius: °C = K − 273.15

Temperature Scales

Celsius (°C) is used in most countries worldwide. Water freezes at 0°C and boils at 100°C at standard atmospheric pressure.

Fahrenheit (°F) is primarily used in the United States. Water freezes at 32°F and boils at 212°F.

Kelvin (K) is the SI unit of temperature used in science. 0 K is absolute zero, the theoretical lowest possible temperature.

Key Reference Points

  • Absolute zero: -273.15 °C = -459.67 °F = 0 K
  • Water freezes: 0 °C = 32 °F = 273.15 K
  • Body temperature: 37 °C = 98.6 °F = 310.15 K
  • Water boils: 100 °C = 212 °F = 373.15 K
